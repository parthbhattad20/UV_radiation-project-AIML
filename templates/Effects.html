Harmful Rays:

• DNA Damage:
• Impact: UV radiation can damage DNA, causing mutations and potentially leading to skin cancer. • Cure: The body has mechanisms to repair DNA, removing and replacing the damaged sections. • DNA Repair Pathways:
• Impact: Too much UV radiation can overwhelm the body’s repair systems, leaving DNA damage unrepaired. • Cure: Medicines or gene therapy can help boost the body’s natural repair processes. • Epigenetic:
• Impact: UV radiation can change how genes are expressed, which can lead to cancer. • Cure: Treatments can reverse these changes to restore normal gene function.
• Signaling:
• Impact: UV radiation can mess up cell communication, leading to uncontrolled cell growth. • Cure: Targeted treatments can correct these signaling issues to help control cell growth.

Hermetic Rays:

• DNA Damage:
• Impact: Small amounts of UV radiation can cause minor DNA damage, which can trigger protective responses in the body.
• Cure: The body responds by increasing DNA repair activities. • DNA Repair Pathways:
• Impact: Controlled UV exposure can strengthen the body’s DNA repair systems. • Cure: Controlled UV exposure or treatments can prepare the body’s repair systems to handle damage better.
• Epigenetic:
• Impact: Mild UV radiation can positively affect gene expression, helping cells respond to stress. • Cure: Controlled exposure or treatments can enhance these protective gene responses.
• Signaling:
• Impact: Controlled UV exposure can activate beneficial cell signaling pathways, improving cell survival and repair. • Cure: Controlled UV exposure or medicines can boost these protective signaling pathways.
